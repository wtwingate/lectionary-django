About

This is a website for the Sunday, Holy Day, and Commemoration Lectionary of the Anglican Church in North America, as found in the Book of Common Prayer 2019.

The primary purpose of this site is to provide a convenient way for pastors, worship leaders, and church administrators to get accurate information about upcoming holy days in the lectionary. In particular, this site provides a number of features that should prove useful for those who are responsible for creating bulletins and Scripture inserts for their local congregations.

Normally, the process looks something like this: check your calendar date for the upcoming Sunday, do some mental arithmetic to determine the corresponding holy day in the lectionary, flip through the Prayer Book to find the designated propers and lessons, search online for the Scripture text, and then do your best to copy and paste all the relevant bits into your document without forgetting or misclicking anything. As someone who has spent countless hours doing so for my own parish, I know firsthand just how tedious and error prone this can be.

How to Use

Most of the site should be fairly self-explanatory. By default, the main page shows all the upcoming holy days over the next two weeks. If you need to look further ahead (or behind) you can set your own start and end dates and click the search button.

Clicking on the name of a holy day will take you to its detail page, where the Collect and Lessons for the day are displayed, along with other bits of liturgical information. If you're making a bulletin or Scripture insert, you can click the big copy button to have all those texts automagically copied into your system clipboard. Copy. Paste. Prosper.

Psalter quotations are taken from the New Coverdale Psalter found in the Prayer Book. Scripture quotations are taken from the English Standard Version of the Bible, since this is the translation used in the Prayer Book. Quotations of the apocrypha are taken from the New Revised Standard Version.
